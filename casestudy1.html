Redesign of WWF's Australia website and mobile version

We provide an end-to-end website and mobile app for donations, volunteers, and adoptions.

Project Overview

Challenge

This project aims to investigate ways to make wildlife conservation more accessible to the general public. To accomplish this objective, the current website needs to be reconstructed and updated with new features.

  • Create a User-Friendly Design
  • Provide Comprehensive Information
  • Simplify Navigation
  • Interactive Features
  • Encourage Donations

01 Research

Research enables me to dig deep into my understanding of users - not only their immediate frustrations, but also their hopes, fears, abilities, limitations, reasoning, and goals. It lays essential foundations for creating solutions in later stages.

Background

WWF-Australia is a part of the global WWF network, and it works to conserve Australia's unique biodiversity and ecosystems. The organization has a range of initiatives and programs aimed at addressing the major environmental challenges facing Australia, such as habitat loss, climate change, and water scarcity

One of the strengths of the WWF website is its clean and intuitive design. The navigation menu is easy to use and provides quick access to key sections of the website, such as news, conservation efforts, and ways to get involved. The website's search function is also effective in helping visitors find specific information.

Research Objectives:

Evaluate the effectiveness of the website's design and layout

Identify opportunities for improving the user experience and engagement

Assess the website's accessibility and usability for different user groups

Identify areas for improvement in the website's content and messaging

Identify user needs and pain points on the WWF website

Research Methods:

Online surveys: To gather quantitative data on user demographics, behavior, and satisfaction with the website.

Interviews: To gather qualitative data on user needs, preferences, and opinions about the website.

Competitor analysis:Competitor analysis: Can help WWF understand the strengths and weaknesses of other non-profit organizations in the environmental protection.

Heuristic evaluation: By analyzing the product based on established usability heuristics, we can gain a more objective perspective on its strengths and weaknesses, and make informed decisions about how to improve its overall usability.

03 Design process

Three features were chosen, including a reward system, ways to support, and transparency.

By using the "I like, I wish, What if" technique, Our team gained valuable insights into how users interact with non-profit organisations or their services, and using the feedback, we might be able to improve the user experience and create a matrix to prioritize features.

Once the features have been prioritized, the team might be able to use the matrix to guide their decision-making and focus on implementing the most important and feasible features first.

Therefore, we have chosen three features - donation, adoption, and volunteering - that we want to redesign and optimize for WWF in order to improve their efficiency and functionality.

04 Prototype & Test

Usability Testing: Rosulate

To evaluate the usability of the prototype, I conducted usability testing with a total of 5 participants. The testing was conducted both in-person and remotely, and I made detailed observations of each participant's interaction with the prototype. For each participant, I created transcripts that captured any mistakes, slips, or confusions they experienced during the testing process. By analyzing these observations, I gained valuable insights into areas where the prototype could be improved to provide a better user experience.

By conducting user testing, we were able to identify areas for improvement and make visual enhancements to the product. Specifically, we added different images, implemented scrolling cards, and upgraded the navigation bar with a hamburger menu. These changes were made with the goal of enhancing the user experience and making the product more visually appealing and user-friendly.

UI style guide

We chose a style guide inspired by the Eucalyptus Forest, with a color palette based on shades of green to represent trees. The background color was black, as it creates an immersive experience that allows users to fully engage with the website and experience a range of emotions.
